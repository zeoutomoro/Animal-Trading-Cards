Cat

A cat’s flexible body and lightning-fast reflexes make it one of the most skilled hunters in the animal world.

  • Scientific Name: Felis catus
  • Length: 46 centimeters (body)
  • Average Lifespan: 12–18 years
  • Habitat: Domestic homes, urban areas, forests, and grasslands

Cats are small, agile mammals known for their sharp senses, quick reflexes, and independent yet affectionate nature. They can see well in low light, jump several times their body length, and communicate using vocal sounds, tail movements, and body language.
